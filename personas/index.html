CS1300 Personas & Storyboarding Assignment 🍩

Introduction 🍨

Personally, I haven't had a great experience with the printers at Brown. I've always wondered if I was the only one who finds the process confusing. Hence, when the opportunity arose for me to decide on a public interface to research, it was an easy decision. I wanted to figure out whether I was the problem, or if not, which aspects of the printing system harm the user experience of students at Brown.

A Sketch of the Interface 🍪

Here is the intended way students are supposed to use the printers at Brown (to the best of my knowledge).

  1. Decide if you want to print black and white or color.
  2. Go online and find a list of all available printers and their locations. If you want to print color, make sure you find a location with color printers.
  3. Go to the physical location and look for the printers. Once found, check the sign on the printer to ensure that it supports color (if you want to print color).
  4. Read the instructions on the top right corner of the printer, and navigate to the given link on your phone or computer.
  5. Upload the file you want to print, indicating how many copies you would like to print.
  6. Swipe your ID card on the reader next to the computer.
  7. Use the computer and select the file you uploaded.
  8. Choose either "print and keep", or "print and delete". Either option prints the number of copies that you specified.
  9. Logout of your account on the computer, and collect your printed file from the printer.

As you can see, the process is pretty complex.

Recording Observations 🍫

To collect real world data, I observed and interviewed three different students who used the printers available in the SciLi. For privacy reasons, their names are anonymous.

Observations on Printer Usage:

Person 1: They walked up to the computer and swiped their ID card on the reader. The screen displayed that they had no files to be printed, and that they needed to upload a file by following the instructions on the printer. Then, they went to the printer and glanced at the bottom left section, before noticing and reading the instructions at the top right corner. They then opened their phone and presumably followed the instructions to upload their file (I did not see their phone screen). Then, they went back to the computer, refreshed the queue, and their file appeared. They clicked "print and delete", and the file was printed out.

Person 2: They walked up to the computer and swiped their ID card on the reader. The screen displayed that their file was ready to be printed, and they clicked "print and delete". Multiple copies of their file were printed out, as they had presumably set the copy count when sending in the print request.

Person 3: They walked up to the printer and briefly read the instructions on the top right corner. Then they left and returned after a few minutes. They then swiped their ID card on the reader, and saw that their file was uploaded. They clicked "print and keep" once, and then "print and delete" afterwards, to get a total of two copies.

Observations on Question Responses

Q1) In general people's opinions were close to neutral, and nobody had extreme opinions.

Q2) The art student used the printer more frequently, but most people seemed to use the printer only every once in a while.

Q3) It's evident that there are issues with the interface. Person 2 fell to the gulf of execution - he wanted to print 100 pages, but the interface made it difficult to do so. Person 3, on the other hand, fell to the gulf of evaluation - there was no clear confirmation that they had uploaded their file, causing them to waste time.

Q4) The instruction page doesn't explain how to use the site to upload your file. Although you can specify color, sided-ness, and size, those options are hidden away and hard to discover. This is clear from their responses.

Q5) Person 2's issue is interesting, as a list of the printers which support color is actually available online. However, isn't it normal to assume that all printers support color (given that the printers look identical)? Should we expect students to go to the trouble of searching online for a specific printer? It seems to me that having clearer physical indications on the printers would have a much greater effect. Person 3's issue is also interesting, which connects to the next question, so I will discuss it there.

Q6) The responses to this question were the most surprising. In fact, there are 26 locations on campus that have a number of printers available. Yet, all respondents claimed that the number of printing locations they knew about were under five. This is further evidence to support that nobody checks the online list of available printers, but instead search for them in places where they need to print most frequently (i.e. SciLi).
